Maison Cartion

Parisian rigor, luminous restraint.

Cartion reimagines heritage savoir-faire with a graphic, monochrome posture. Sculpted volumes, knife-edge facets, and mirror-bright settings echo the maison's devotion to clarity and balance.

Each creation is drafted in charcoal on vellum, cast in recycled precious metals, and set by hand. The result: objects that speak softly yet command the room.

Process

Savoir-faire in four movements.

  1. 01

    Sketch & proportion

    Architectural sketches define balance, negative space, and how light will travel across each plane.

  2. 02

    Lost-wax casting

    Recycled precious metals are poured into precision molds for crisp edges and structural integrity.

  3. 03

    Setting & polish

    Master setters secure stones with tension or micro-prongs; surfaces are polished to liquid mirror.

  4. 04

    Final appraisal

    Each piece endures a 40-point inspection to certify brilliance, comfort, and lifelong endurance.
